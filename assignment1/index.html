Interactive Experience Review

1) What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

The first thing I noticed was the minimal 3d designs that are interactive with my mouse censor and the smooth animation of each categories when i hover my mouse on it. The color palette was also very appealing and family-friendly with a blue, purple and pink theme. The name “Luni” was front and center, and the simple navigation made it clear that this wasn’t a cluttered site it was designed to be straightforward and to the point.

2) Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions.

  1. Exploring different sections like "Studio," "Omada," "Career," and "Luni life”
  2. Interacting with different objects in each sections (balls, punching bag, pans”
  3. Take a deeper look of the section in the “discover” page
  4. Accessing external links to LinkedIn and Twitter.

3) What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

I think the most engaging part is where i can play around with the balls in the “Omada” section. The activity is surprisingly addictive and there is even a leaderboard for the users that score the goals.

4) What was the most common action in your two minute interaction with the experience?

Scrolling and navigating between sections. The website is designed in a way that encourages browsing rather than deep interaction. I found myself clicking on a section, skimming through, and then moving to the next one to see if there was more information. Messing around with the interactable objects in sections was also really fun.

5) What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

It feels like the main goal of the website is to showcase Luni’s brand, giving a polished first impression to potential clients, employees, or partners. It’s not a deep dive into their work but more of a high-level introduction to who they are and what they do. It seems like they want to establish credibility quickly rather than overwhelm visitors with too much detail.

6) How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

The site communicates this goal by keeping things minimal and visually appealing. There’s no unnecessary clutter—just a few key sections that introduce Luni and its work. The sleek design, smooth scrolling, and subtle animations all reinforce the idea that Luni is a forward-thinking company.

7) What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time? (For how long and how many different times)

It feels like a site you visit once or twice to get an overview of the company. Maybe you come back if you’re looking for a job or want to check for updates, but it doesn’t feel like something you’d revisit regularly. It’s designed more as a digital business card than an interactive platform.
