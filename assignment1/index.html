Interactive Experience Review

1) What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

The first thing I noticed was the minimal 3d designs that are interactive with my mouse censor and the smooth animation of each categories when i hover my mouse on it. The color palette was also very appealing and family-friendly with a blue, purple and pink theme. The name “Luni” was front and center, and the simple navigation made it clear that this wasn’t a cluttered site it was designed to be straightforward and to the point.

  1. Exploring different sections like "Studio," "Omada," "Career," and "Luni life”
  2. Interacting with different objects in each sections (balls, punching bag, pans”
  3. Take a deeper look of the section in the “discover” page
  4. Accessing external links to LinkedIn and Twitter.

3) What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

I think the most engaging part of the website is the interactive feature in the "Omada" section, where you can play around with the balls. The activity is surprisingly addictive, and it’s clear that a lot of thought went into making it feel smooth and enjoyable. I wasn’t expecting to find a mini-game on a professional-looking site like this, but it was a fun surprise that kept me engaged for much longer than I anticipated. What makes this feature particularly interesting is that it isn’t just a random interactive element, it’s tied to competition and engagement through the leaderboard.

4) What was the most common action in your two minute interaction with the experience?

Scrolling and navigating between sections was my most common action throughout the experience. The website is designed in a way that naturally encourages browsing rather than deep interaction. The clean and minimal layout makes it easy to move from one section to another without feeling lost or overwhelmed. Instead of diving into lengthy text-heavy pages, I found myself clicking on a section, quickly skimming through the content, and then moving to the next one to see what else the site had to offer. This created a fluid, almost exploratory experience, where I was constantly on the lookout for new visuals, animations, or interactive elements.

5) What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

It feels like the main goal of the website is to showcase Luni’s brand in a way that leaves a strong, polished first impression. The design, structure, and content all seem carefully curated to communicate professionalism, creativity, and modernity. Rather than being a deep dive into the specifics of their work, the site functions as a high-level introduction to who they are, what they do, and what they stand for. It’s an elevator pitch in website form, not too overwhelming, but just enough to spark curiosity and establish credibility. For potential employees, the website serves as a recruitment tool, showcasing the company as a modern and exciting place to work. The presence of the “Career” section hints at growth and opportunity, while the interactive and playful elements (like the "Omada" game) add a layer of personality to the company.

6) How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

The site effectively communicates this goal by maintaining a minimalist and visually striking design that immediately conveys professionalism and modernity. Instead of overwhelming visitors with excessive text, dense information, or a cluttered interface, the website strategically presents just a few key sections that introduce Luni and its work in a concise yet compelling way. This ensures that users can absorb the most important aspects of the brand without feeling bogged down by unnecessary details. One of the most noticeable ways the website achieves this is through its sleek and polished design. The layout is clean and well-structured, allowing each section to breathe. White space is used effectively to create a sense of openness, and this contributes to an overall feeling of sophistication and intentionality. The limited use of text ensures that visitors focus more on the visuals and interactive elements rather than having to read through long paragraphs.

7) What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time? (For how long and how many different times)

The website feels like a place you visit once or twice to get a clear, high-level overview of the company rather than a platform designed for repeated visits. Its minimalist approach and straight-to-the-point content suggest that its primary function is to provide a polished first impression rather than to serve as a continuously updated or content-rich destination. There isn’t an extensive blog, regularly refreshed news updates, or community-driven interactions that would encourage frequent returns.

8) How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

The site doesn’t really invite repeat visits. There’s no blog, no regularly updated content, nothing interactive that might keep someone coming back. It feels more like a one-time stop — something you look at to get a feel for who or what Luni is, and then move on. The only real prompts for ongoing connection are the links to LinkedIn and Twitter, which suggests that if you’re interested in updates or staying connected, that’s where you should go. In that way, it feels like the site is designed more as a polished intro than something built for long-term engagement.

The whole look and feel of the site reminds me of modern startup websites — especially in tech or design. It’s very clean, with lots of white space, nice typography, and smooth transitions. There’s a bit of that “Apple vibe” minimal but intentional. It also feels a bit like a digital portfolio crossed with a networking profile. The LinkedIn and Twitter links make it clear this is aimed at a professional audience, not casual browsers. Altogether, it gives off a sleek, business-focused energy.

The attention to detail, from the clean layout to the smooth animations and refined typography, signals that they take their work seriously and value high-quality presentation. 13) What is the most satisfying part of the interaction to you and what makes that part satisfying?

The design itself was the most satisfying part. The site is smooth, visually appealing, and easy to navigate. There’s no overwhelming text, no clunky UI just a well-crafted digital experience. It feels high-end and well thought out, which makes a strong impression.
